How Many Calories Does a Half Marathon Burn?

A half marathon (21.1 km / 13.1 miles) burns approximately 1,200–2,100 kcal depending on body weight. The reliable rule: 1 kcal per kg per km. A 70 kg runner burns roughly 70 × 21.1 = 1,477 kcal — nearly a full day's worth of calories for a moderately active person.

Half Marathon vs 10K Calories

A half marathon burns roughly 2.1× more calories than a 10K at the same pace and weight. If you burn 700 kcal for a 10K, expect ~1,450 kcal for a half marathon. The extra distance also elevates post-race calorie burn (EPOC) for 12–24 hours afterward.

Do You Need to Fuel During a Half Marathon?

For most runners, yes — especially those finishing in over 90 minutes. Your body stores roughly 1,500–2,000 kcal of glycogen. A half marathon can deplete this entirely. Taking 1–2 energy gels or chews (100–200 kcal each) from around 45 minutes in helps maintain pace and prevents hitting the wall.

Half Marathon Calorie Burn by Pace

While distance is the main driver, pace affects the MET value. A 70 kg runner finishes a half marathon in ~2:20 at easy pace (9 km/h) burning ~1,440 kcal, vs ~1:35 at fast pace (13 km/h) burning ~1,350 kcal — slightly less because higher pace has shorter duration. Either way, it's a massive calorie burn.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many calories does a half marathon burn?
Between 1,200–2,100 kcal depending on body weight. A 70 kg runner burns ~1,450 kcal. The rule: 1 kcal per kg per km — so 70 × 21.1 ≈ 1,477 kcal.
Can running a half marathon help lose weight?
Yes — significantly. One half marathon burns 1,200–2,100 kcal. That's roughly 0.35–0.6 lb of fat per race. Training for a half marathon typically results in 3–6 lb of fat loss over 12 weeks.
What should I eat the night before a half marathon?
Carbohydrate-rich meals 12–15 hours before the race: pasta, rice, bread, or potatoes. Aim for 8–10g of carbs per kg of bodyweight the day before. Avoid high-fat, high-fibre, or unfamiliar foods.
How many calories should I eat after a half marathon?
Prioritize carbs + protein within 30–60 minutes of finishing: 1–1.2g carbs per kg bodyweight + 20–30g protein. Then eat a full recovery meal within 2–3 hours to replenish your 1,200–2,100 kcal deficit.
