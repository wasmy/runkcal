Treadmill Calorie Calculator — How It Works

Our treadmill calorie calculator uses the MET (Metabolic Equivalent of Task) formula adjusted for both speed and incline. Unlike outdoor running calculators, treadmill workouts can vary significantly based on gradient — which is why incline is a key input here.

Does Treadmill Incline Burn More Calories?

Significantly yes. Each 1% of incline increases calorie burn by approximately 5–10%. At 5% incline, you burn roughly 40% more calories than at flat grade for the same speed and duration. The "12-3-30" method (12% incline, 3 mph, 30 min) burns 250–350 kcal depending on body weight.

Treadmill vs Outdoor Running — Calorie Difference

At 0% incline, a treadmill burns about 5–10% fewer calories than outdoor running at the same speed, because there's no wind resistance or terrain variation. Setting the treadmill to 1% incline compensates for this, making calorie burn equivalent to outdoor running.

The MET Formula for Treadmill

Formula: kcal = (MET_base + incline_bonus) × weight (kg) × duration (hours) × sex_factor. MET ranges from 3.5 (slow walk) to 14+ (sprinting). Incline adds approximately 0.15 MET per 1% grade. Our calculator uses calibrated values from published exercise science data.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many calories does a treadmill burn per hour?
A 70 kg person burns approximately 300–830 kcal/hour depending on speed and incline. Walking at 5 km/h flat burns ~300 kcal/hour; running at 12 km/h burns ~720 kcal/hour.
Does incline on treadmill burn more calories?
Yes — significantly. Each 1% incline adds 5–10% more calorie burn. A 5% incline can increase total calories burned by 30–50% vs flat walking at the same speed.
How long on treadmill to burn 500 calories?
At 10 km/h (1% incline), a 70 kg person burns 500 kcal in about 50 minutes. At walking pace (5 km/h), it takes around 95–100 minutes. Use our calculator above for your exact numbers.
Is the treadmill calorie counter accurate?
Treadmill built-in counters overestimate by 10–20% because they often don't account for your actual weight. Our MET-based calculator using your real weight and incline gives a more accurate estimate.
What is the 12-3-30 treadmill workout calories?
The 12-3-30 method (12% incline, 3 mph / ~5 km/h, 30 min) burns approximately 250–380 kcal depending on body weight. A 70 kg person burns around 310 kcal using this method.
