Walking Calorie Calculator — How It Works

Our walking calorie calculator uses the MET (Metabolic Equivalent of Task) formula to estimate kilocalories burned during any walk. Enter your weight, distance, and pace for an instant result.

How Many Calories Does Walking Burn?

Walking burns around 40–60 kcal per km depending on your weight and speed. A 70 kg person walking at normal pace (~4 km/h) burns approximately 50 kcal per km — about 250 kcal for a 5km walk.

Walking vs Running Calories

Running burns roughly 2x more calories per minute than walking. However, per kilometer, the difference is smaller — running burns about 70–80 kcal/km vs 40–60 kcal/km walking. Use our running calorie calculator to compare.

The MET Formula for Walking

Formula: kcal = MET × weight (kg) × time (hours). Walking MET ranges from 2.5 (slow stroll) to 5.0 (very brisk walk). Our calculator uses 4 calibrated pace levels.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many calories does walking burn per km?
On average 40-60 kcal per km. A 70 kg person burns ~50 kcal/km at normal walking pace.
How many steps burn 100 calories?
Roughly 2,000–2,500 steps burn about 100 calories, depending on your weight and stride length.
Is walking enough to lose weight?
Yes — walking 30 min/day at brisk pace burns ~150-200 kcal. Combined with a calorie deficit, it supports steady weight loss.
Does walking speed affect calories burned?
Yes — brisk walking (5-6 km/h) burns 20-30% more calories than slow walking (3 km/h) for the same distance.
