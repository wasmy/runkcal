How Many Calories Does a Marathon Burn?

A marathon (42.2 km / 26.2 miles) burns approximately 2,500–4,200 kcal depending on body weight. The reliable rule: 1 kcal per kg per km. A 70 kg runner burns roughly 70 × 42.2 = 2,954 kcal — the equivalent of an entire day's food intake in a single event.

Why Runners "Hit the Wall" at Mile 20

Your body stores only 1,800–2,000 kcal of glycogen (muscle + liver). A marathon burns 2,500–4,000+ kcal. At some point — usually around km 30–35 (mile 18–21) — glycogen runs out and the body shifts to fat oxidation, which is 30–40% slower. This is "hitting the wall." The fix: consume 60–90g of carbs per hour from gels or sports drinks starting at km 10.

Marathon vs Half Marathon Calories

A full marathon burns approximately twice the calories of a half marathon — 2,900 vs 1,450 kcal for a 70 kg runner. However, post-race EPOC (afterburn) is also much higher, potentially adding 200–400 extra kcal over the following 24 hours.

Marathon Nutrition Strategy

2–3 days before: Carb-load at 8–10g/kg/day. Race morning: 200–400 kcal carbs, 2–3 hours before start. During the race: 1 energy gel (100 kcal) every 30–45 minutes from km 10 onward = 6–9 gels total. After: 1–1.5g carbs/kg + 25–30g protein within 30 minutes of finishing.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many calories does running a marathon burn?
Between 2,500–4,200 kcal depending on body weight. The rule: 1 kcal per kg per km. A 70 kg runner burns ~2,950 kcal for a full 42.2 km marathon.
Why do I gain weight after a marathon?
Temporary water retention from glycogen replenishment (each gram of glycogen stores 3g of water) and inflammation causes a 1–3 kg weight spike in the days following a marathon. It resolves within 5–7 days.
How many gels do I need for a marathon?
Typically 6–10 gels (100 kcal each) for a 3:30–5:00 finish. Take one every 30–45 min starting at km 10. This replaces ~600–1,000 kcal of the ~2,900 kcal burned — enough to prevent glycogen depletion and the wall.
How long does it take to lose the weight from training for a marathon?
Marathon training burns an extra 1,500–3,000 kcal/week. Over a 16-week plan that's 24,000–48,000 extra kcal burned — equivalent to 3–6 kg of fat, assuming diet stays the same. Most runners lose 2–4 kg during training.
